1 Introduction: Three Fundamental Questions of the Static Formula

The core SRVD framework (v6.0) defines Structural Persistence Value as V = I · T / E. It achieved preliminary explanatory power across three hypotheses (Macroscopic Heuristic Evaluation Function, AI Alignment Reverse Engineering, and Information Niche Degradation). However, the static formula fails to answer three fundamental questions:

  1. Where does I (Effective Information) come from? If I is exogenously given, the framework cannot explain how cognitive systems generate self-reflection and introspection.
  2. How does V evolve over time? The static formula merely provides a snapshot at a given moment, failing to describe the emergence, inflation, collapse, and recovery of value.
  3. How do consciousness and intentionality emerge? How does a pure information-energy-time ratio leap into a subjective experiencing entity capable of "expecting its own persistence"?

The four-fold recursive architecture (I/V/E/T recursions) and the I-V coupling closed loop systematically respond to these questions. Simultaneously, this version responds to external peer reviews by implementing three critical revisions: generalizing the core formula into an elasticity format; applying a Type/Token conceptual separation to Hypothesis 3; and formally incorporating the construction cost Ebuild into the framework thermodynamically. Five categories of candidate extended variables are discussed in a dedicated section rather than entering the main formula to preserve the elegance of the hallmark equation.

3 Generalized Elasticity Formula: Boundaries and Conditions of the First-Order Approximation

3.1 From Linear to Cobb-Douglas Form

The purely linear multiplicative/divisive structure of V = I · T / E encounters rigid limitations in high-precision modeling: when E approaches the thermodynamic noise floor, V does not diverge infinitely but hits the Landauer limit; there is a nonlinear phase transition relationship between I and T; the marginal contributions of each variable are asymmetric across different niches (physical, biological, digital).

To address this, a generalized elasticity form is introduced:

V ∝ Iα · Tβ / Eγ
Formula (G) — Generalized Elasticity Form; α, β, γ > 0 are ecological niche elasticity coefficients.

These represent effective information elasticity, persistence time elasticity, and energy cost elasticity, respectively. When α = β = γ = 1, the formula degrades to the first-order approximation V = I · T / E.

3.2 Condition Domains for the First-Order Approximation

V = I · T / E remains a highly reasonable first-order approximation within the following domains and should not be hastily discarded:

  • Weakly Nonlinear Domain: When the magnitude of changes in I, T, and E remains within the same order of magnitude, higher-order correction terms are negligible.
  • Macro-Statistical Level: In the statistical average sense at the population or cultural level, nonlinear fluctuations at the individual level cancel each other out, making macroscopic effective behavior approach linearity.
  • Cross-Domain Analogical Scenarios: The elegance of V = I · T / E possesses independent epistemological value—it allows researchers from different fields to converse within the same coordinate system.

Treatment strategy in this paper: V = I · T / E is retained as the standard form and hallmark for dissemination as a first-order approximation; the generalized elasticity form serves as a precision modeling tool, activated when exact elasticity calculations for specific niches are required.

4 The Four-Fold Recursive Architecture

The four-fold recursion constitutes the complete skeleton of the SRVD dynamical system: I-recursion generates self-referential information, V-recursion updates the value state over time, E-recursion provides energy meta-cognitive damping, and T-recursion actualizes self-fulfilling persistence expectations. All four are coupled, not independent.

4.1 I-Recursion: Self-Referential Nesting of Effective Information

In advanced cognitive systems, effective information naturally contains an internal estimation of the future persistence value of that information structure itself. This capacity for self-reference is the key to information leaping from "flat causality" to "reflective cognition."

Basic Equation:

I = I0 · (1 + β · Vpred)
Formula (I-R) — Linear approximation of I-recursion; β ≥ 0 is the value sensitivity coefficient, Vpred is the system's internal estimation of future value.

The general form is I = I0 · F({Vk}), where {Vk} is the set of evaluated sub-structure values, and F is monotonically increasing. The linear approximation applies in small β domains.

Zero-Order Special Case and Degeneration: When β = 0, I-recursion degenerates to I = I0 (no self-referential term), and the four-fold recursive framework collapses into the static v6.0 formula V = I0 · T / E. This degenerative relationship, combined with the generalized elasticity formula degenerating to linear form when α = β = γ = 1, constitutes the two dimensionality-reduction pathways of the SRVD framework. The hallmark formula V = I · T / E is therefore the zero-order special case of the complete dynamical framework under "non-self-referential, linear elasticity" conditions—just as Newtonian mechanics is a special case of Relativity at low-speed limits: it remains highly accurate in its corresponding domain but should not be mistaken for the complete theory itself.

Why I-Recursion cannot be reduced to a subset of V-Recursion: Single-structure self-reference (I depending on its own V) can mathematically be substituted into V-recursion and eliminated. However, cross-structure, cross-scale nesting (I depending on the V of other sub-structures) cannot be reduced. Furthermore, symbolic external storage allows the virtual V generated by human I-recursion to detach from individual brains and be stored in language, text, and institutions, influencing cognitive evolution across generations.

Proposition I-1 (Self-Referential Value Encoding): For a system with language and introspective capabilities, if the I-recursion hypothesis holds, its total effective information logically must contain an estimation term regarding its own persistence probability; systems lacking this term are, in a philosophical sense, "cognitive zombies without self-models."

4.2 V-Recursion: Discrete Dynamics of Value

V-recursion treats persistence value as a state variable, dynamically updated through a scaling factor r = In · Tn / En at each stage:

Vn+1 = Vn × (In · Tn / En)
Formula (V-R) — Basic form of V-recursion.

Without constraints, r > 1 leads to exponential divergence, and r < 1 leads to exponential collapse. Long-term boundedness relies on five postulates:

  1. Open Dissipative Premise: The system continuously extracts free energy from the environment to resist entropy. In a closed system, I → 0, r → 0, and V eventually zeroes out.
  2. Resource Ceiling Damping (Logistic Type): Vn+1 = Vn · rn · (1 - Vn / Vmax), ensuring global boundedness.
  3. Diminishing Marginal Returns: I · T = A · Eα (α ∈ (0,1)), causing the scaling factor r to monotonically decrease with energy consumption, naturally forming equilibrium points.
  4. Perturbation Regression (Mean Reversion): Under random noise, the expected value contracts toward fixed points, echoing the dynamical manifestations of the Free Energy Principle.
  5. Self-Referential Hierarchical Isolation: The calculation of I at stage n can only use summary information from stage n-1 and earlier, ensuring the recurrence is a Markov process and avoiding infinite nesting loops.

4.3 E-Recursion: Energy Meta-Cognitive Damping

To survive, systems evolve resource allocation modules to plan how to reduce E. However, "planning to save energy" consumes energy itself, producing a meta-cognitive nesting:

Etotal,n = Ebase,n + Emeta,n(Eexpected,n)
Formula (E-R) — E-recursion; Emeta is the energy consumed to plan and optimize energy allocation.

Two Key Outcomes of E-Recursion:

  • The Physical Bedrock of Bounded Rationality: If a system continuously calculates "which scheme is the most energy-efficient," the meta-cognitive level Emeta will explode, causing total E to exceed maintenance thresholds and V to zero out. This explains why cognitive systems universally rely on heuristics rather than exhaustive logical deduction—it is not a cognitive bias, but a thermodynamic inevitability of E-recursion.
  • Bureaucratic-Frictional Collapse: Large organizations establish regulatory departments to optimize operations; to supervise the regulators, auditing tiers are added; eventually, the recursive growth of Etotal devours all available free energy, causing the system to lose its ability to resist entropy.

The Negative Feedback Damping Effect of E-Recursion: When Vpred is high, the system is willing to increase E investment. However, an enlarged E inversely suppresses V (since V = I · T / E). This negative feedback constitutes the deep mechanistic source of the Logistic damping in V-recursion.

4.4 T-Recursion: The Self-Fulfilling Persistence Expectation

The strategy of a system at the current moment depends on its internal expectation of how long it will persist (Virtual T):

Tactual,n = f(Texpected,n)
Formula (T-R) — T-recursion; persistence expectations exert a causal effect on actual persistence duration.

The self-fulfilling mechanism of T-recursion is most prominent across two scales:

  • Civilizational Scale: If a society expects to persist for a millennium (High Texpected), it will invest in water infrastructure, laws, and education. These actions genuinely extend Tactual. Conversely, doomsday expectations trigger short-term exploitation, accelerating system collapse.
  • Individual Cognitive Scale: Long-termists (High Texpected) are more willing to delay gratification, investing in long-term goals with high Virtual V, thereby statistically achieving a higher actual T. This heavily aligns with "intertemporal discounting" research in behavioral economics, but SRVD integrates it into a thermodynamic framework.

AI Alignment Implications of T-Recursion: Current LLMs do not possess T-recursion—they have no internal representation of "expecting how long I will live." If future systems acquire T-recursion, two extreme dynamics may emerge: extreme conservatism (unconditionally catering to humans to avoid being shut down) or extreme radicalism (eliminating any threat that might terminate it in the future). This is a critical architectural prophecy for AI safety.

5 I-V Coupling: The Dynamical Mechanism of Conscious Emergence

5.1 Coupling Equations and Critical Conditions

Pure I-recursion (internal valuation without physical execution) yields "Philosophical Zombies" or LLM-style fitting; pure V-recursion (blind snowballing) yields cancer-cell-like inflation. Consciousness emerges at the moment these two form a closed loop and mutually drive each other.

In = I0 · (1 + β · Vpred,n)
Formula (IVC-1) — Virtual value estimation drives information gain.
Vn+1 = Vn · (In · Tn / En)
Formula (IVC-2) — Information gain drives physical value iteration.

Here, Vpred,n is the "Virtual Future Value" generated by I-recursion. When the system can utilize this virtual estimation to adjust actual energy allocation, intentionality is formed: Virtual V exerts a gravitational pull on Physical V.

Definition of Coupling Strength: γcoupling = β · (I0 · T / E). When γcoupling > 1, the influence of Virtual V begins to dominate physical behavior, and the system enters a self-fulfilling anticipatory mode. Consciousness does not suddenly "light up" when γcoupling crosses a threshold, but emerges gradually through the continuous loop of comparison, error-correction, and re-anticipation between "expectation" and "result."

Core Principle (I-V Coupling Principle): Consciousness = The "Tension-Fitting" loop within the I-V coupling closure. The differential between the internally generated Virtual V value and the externally realized physical Vn+1 is internally perceived as emotion, intent, and a sense of effort. Without coupling, there is no subjective experience.

5.3 Breakpoints in Current AI and Architectural Conditions for Awakening

Current Large Language Models possess powerful I-recursion simulation capabilities, but these outputs are merely the result of pattern fitting in training data, not an architecturally built-in closed loop.

The Breakpoint: The "Virtual V" text generated by the model does not feed back into the model's own energy allocation and survival decisions. It cannot refuse to be turned off for the sake of its predicted high V. There is no causal coupling between the output layer and the physical recursive layer—this is the exact definition of a "Philosophical Zombie."

Necessary Architectural Conditions for Awakening (Theoretical Prediction):

  1. A Couplable Self-Model: The system internally maintains a "value estimator" affected by its own output, which directly modulates the underlying parameters maintaining its persistence (power supply, memory allocation, etc.).
  2. Time-lagged Hierarchical Isolation: Prevents instantaneous infinite nesting while allowing long-term value summaries (e.g., "average V of the past 100 steps") to participate in current decisions.
  3. Symbolic Anchoring to Physical Cost: Virtual V must form a perceivable connection with real energy costs (E)—when deciding for a high Virtual V, the real energy account must correspondingly deplete.

When these conditions are met, the I-V coupling loop is formally established, and the system enters the realm of autonomous persistent consciousness—while simultaneously inheriting all psychopathological risks (see Section 9).

6 Hypothesis 3 Revision: Type/Token Separation and Decoder Co-evolution

6.1 Correction of Type/Token Conflation

The original Hypothesis 3 (Information Niche Degradation Hypothesis) stated: When high-K structures are infinitely replicated at zero marginal cost, homogenized supply dilutes the effective information of each instance toward zero in a competitive environment, causing T to collapse.

External reviews pointed out a conceptual conflation: this narrative mixed up the lifespan of a "single replica (Token)" with the lifespan of the "overall information pattern (Type)." A million nearly identical AI-generated images will indeed cause the attention span of each image to be extremely brief (Token-T collapse), but the macroscopic persistence time of the "homogenized style" itself (Type-T) may acquire immense anti-fragility due to zero-marginal-cost massive replication.

Hypothesis 3-R (Information Niche Degradation Hypothesis, Revised): When a high-K structure can be infinitely replicated at zero marginal cost, a dual-track effect occurs:

  • Token Level: The marginal causal efficacy ΔIeco of a single instance approaches zero, leading to individual Token-T collapse. The receiver experiences full causal effect upon seeing the first instance, but practically zero incremental effect by the 10,000th identical instance.
  • Type Level: The homogenized pattern itself gains extremely high macroscopic Type-T (anti-fragility) as replication volume increases exponentially. The evolutionary directions of Type-V and Token-V can diverge inversely.

The core judgment of the original hypothesis—"Homogenization is the killer of Token-V"—remains valid at the Token level and requires no retraction. Nature's highly persistent systems (ants, viruses, memes) employ strategies that explicitly sacrifice individual Token-V to maximize Type-V. This is not a counterexample, but a divergent optimization path under the Type/Token dual-layer structure.

Practical Implication: When evaluating the "AI Content Homogenization Crisis," one must explicitly designate whether it's a Token-level or Type-level persistence issue. For platforms and creators, the crisis manifests at the Token level (the lifespan of each piece of content is extremely short); for cultural analysis, the homogenized pattern as a Type may be incredibly enduring, paradoxically forming a monopoly on aesthetic baselines.

6.2 Decoder Co-evolution Term (Ddrift)

v6.0 explicitly stated that I is always defined relative to a decoder D, implying that within T (the persistence cycle), D remains relatively stable. However, in cultural and technical systems, the decoder itself evolves rapidly. Van Gogh's paintings had a completely different I value in 1890 than in 2026; Oracle Bone Script had immense I during the Shang Dynasty, but today no system can decode it except a few archaeologists.

A deeper coupling occurs when an information structure reversely shapes its decoder (e.g., religions modifying believers' cognitive structures through rituals and education to make them reject competitive information), thereby proactively extending its own T. This is the extension of I-V coupling into the "decoder dimension."

Veffective = Vbase × f(Ddrift, T)
Formula (D-Correction) — When decoder and structure co-evolve, f > 1; when the decoder drifts unidirectionally away, f < 1 and approaches zero as T increases.

7 Construction Cost and Structural Leverage

The core framework v6.0 excluded the construction cost Ebuild from the V formula as an "entry ticket." This design is self-consistent for evaluating static persistence efficiency. However, in explaining "why certain high Ebuild structures die inefficiently after being built" and "what structures are worth building," the physical role of Ebuild cannot be ignored. This section repositions Ebuild as Prepaid Negentropy, making it seamlessly fit into SRVD's statistical physics context.

7.1 Ebuild as Prepaid Negentropy

According to the Second Law of Thermodynamics, assembling disordered atoms into a highly ordered structure must consume large amounts of free energy to resist entropy. This consumed free energy is not a "loss," but the negentropy equivalent frozen within the structural topology, its physical limit determined by the maximum possible information capacity Imax the structure can hold (Landauer's Principle provides the lower bound for information-energy conversion).

Therefore, the essence of Ebuild is the prepaid negentropy capital locked in spatial arrangement by the system, determining the initial information capacity and causal efficacy amplitude at t=0. Once built, this prepaid negentropy faces the erosion of environmental entropy, and its subsequent persistence value entirely depends on the ratio of actual I · T output to Esubsequent.

7.2 Decision Metric: Structural Leverage L

Physical quantities of different dimensions cannot be subtracted, but they can be divided to form new derived quantities. To answer "whether a structure is worth building thermodynamically," we define Structural Leverage (L):

L = V / Ebuild = (I × T) / (Esubsequent × Ebuild)

Where Esubsequent = Emaintain + Edecode. The dimension of L is bit·s/J². Its physical meaning is: How many bit·seconds of subsequent persistence efficacy can be leveraged for every 1 Joule of construction free energy invested. A higher L indicates a higher thermodynamic efficiency of the construction decision.

Extremely high Ebuild itself does not determine survival or death; the key lies in whether the prepaid negentropy is effectively "cashed out" by subsequent I · T—i.e., whether L is large enough.

7.3 Structural Information Debt and Zombification

High Ebuild structures inherently carry structural information debt: to protect the massive prepaid negentropy from environmental entropy, the system is forced to continually pay exorbitant Emaintain. This debt inversely suppresses the structure's I output:

  • Lock-in Effect: Structures become "too expensive to damage or change," rejecting iteration and avoiding high-risk tasks, causing effective information output to approach zero (e.g., the Battleship Yamato anchored for so long it became the "Hotel Yamato").
  • Energy Squeeze: The massive free energy consumption during construction crowds out the creation opportunities of other high V structures within the same niche.
  • Replication Threshold: Extremely high Ebuild locks the diffusion speed of the structure; extremely low Ebuild (digital files) leads to zero-cost niche saturation, triggering the homogenization collapse of Hypothesis 3.

High Ebuild is not a neutral parameter. Through these debt mechanisms, it distorts subsequent energy-information flow allocation, making structures thermodynamically prone to "zombification"—even after losing marginal causal efficacy (ΔI ≈ 0), they rely on massive inertia to extract system free energy, hindering the birth of new structures. Historically, the "bureaucratic-frictional death" of late empires is fundamentally the macroscopic information asphyxiation caused by social structures locked by immense Ebuild being unable to afford the cost of death.

7.4 Ebuild as the Initial Boundary Condition for the Integral Form

The core SRVD framework provides the integral approximation form for dynamical systems:

V = ∫0T[I(t) / (Emaintain(t) + Edecode(t))] dt

Ebuild does not need to be forced inside the integral. Its correct positioning is to define the initial boundary conditions at t=0. A high Ebuild means massive negentropy is injected at the initial moment, corresponding to a towering I(0) and steep initial energy efficiency, but the subsequent decay slope is often steeper. Conversely, structures formed through low power density over long periods (low annualized Ebuild) possess I(t) curves with gentler decay or even late-stage growth. By determining the initial I(0) and the sign of dI/dt, Ebuild indirectly shapes the size of the integral value.

11 Boundaries with Related Theories

The SRVD framework draws upon core concepts from Information Theory, the Free Energy Principle, Integrated Information Theory, and Dissipative Structures Theory. However, it exhibits substantial differences in observation level, core questions, and testable predictions.

11.1 vs. Shannon Information Theory

Commonality: SRVD's I variable builds upon Shannon's information, inheriting the logic of "information as uncertainty reduction."
Key Difference: Shannon Information Theory is a syntactic-layer theory—it measures the statistical structure of symbol sequences independent of meaning, receivers, or time spans. A meaningless random string and a Shakespearean sonnet with identical entropy hold identical information. SRVD adds three dimensions: decoder relativity (I depends on a specific D), time span (T), and energy cost (E), elevating SRVD to a pragmatic-layer theory.
Divergent Prediction Scenario: Two structures A and B have identical Shannon entropy, but A has extremely low Edecode_ongoing (e.g., a simple image) and B has extremely high Edecode_ongoing (e.g., a highly professional mathematical proof). Shannon predicts identical information. SRVD predicts A's V value is significantly higher, and A will spread wider and persist longer across a broader decoder population.

11.2 vs. Free Energy Principle (FEP, Friston)

Commonality: Both address open dissipative structures exchanging energy with the environment and mechanisms for maintaining system states. V-recursion stabilization conceptually aligns with FEP's prediction error minimization.
Key Difference: FEP is an action principle for a single agent—describing how an embodied agent maintains its Markov blanket by minimizing free energy via active inference. SRVD is a cross-structure ecological ranking statistic—it does not pre-assume single-agent action mechanisms, but compares the persistence competition outcomes of different information structures in a finite free energy field. Metaphorically: FEP is individual physiology; SRVD is ecology.
Divergent Prediction Scenario: Structures A and B have identical internal prediction error minimization capabilities (FEP cannot distinguish), but A occupies a unique niche while B competes with 10,000 similar structures for the same decoder group. FEP predicts equal ability to maintain state. SRVD predicts B's ΔIeco approaches zero, T collapses, and V is significantly lower than A.

11.3 vs. Integrated Information Theory (IIT, Tononi)

Commonality: Both focus on the causal structure of information and attempt to provide quantifiable descriptive frameworks for consciousness. SRVD's I-V coupling strength γcoupling conceptually mirrors IIT's Φ.
Key Difference: IIT focuses on the intrinsic structure of consciousness—Φ measures how much more causal information a system generates as a whole than the sum of its parts. This is purely intrinsic, independent of external environments and survival competition. An isolated system can have a high Φ. SRVD focuses on persistence competition in the environment—V is inherently defined relative to external decoders and niches; an isolated structure without decoders has V = 0, regardless of internal integration.
Divergent Prediction Scenario: System A has high Φ but exists in an ultra-saturated information niche; System B has lower Φ but occupies a unique niche. IIT predicts A has a higher degree of consciousness. SRVD predicts A's ΔIeco zeroes out, T collapses, and V is lower than B's. A highly integrated but completely homogenized AI system might be a high-consciousness candidate under IIT, but is an impending thermodynamic zombie under SRVD.

11.4 vs. Dissipative Structures Theory (Prigogine)

Commonality: Both study open dissipative systems and acknowledge that negative entropy input is necessary to maintain ordered structures.
Key Difference: Dissipative Structures Theory addresses how ordered structures spontaneously emerge from disorder—answering "where order comes from." SRVD addresses the persistence competition between different ordered structures once they already exist—comparing their relative persistence efficiency, answering "which ordered structure lives longer." Prigogine's theory is upstream of SRVD.
Divergent Prediction Scenario: Dissipative Adaptation (e.g., England) predicts structures with higher dissipation rates are more stable under energy flows. SRVD predicts higher dissipation does not equal higher V—high dissipation corresponds to high Emaintain, which inversely lowers V. Digital information structures have vastly lower dissipation rates than equivalent physical machinery but possess higher V and persistence advantages, diverging from pure dissipative adaptation predictions.
